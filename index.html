The lack of Critical and Creative Thinking in India: Why is that?


This question has crossed my mind like a lot of times, why is it that we are best suited for, following orders?
I think since our childhood we have been conditioned that an ideal person is an obedient and soft person. One who follows orders and never fights no matter what. Whenever a child, whether at home or in school tried to stand up for himself or made a decision, he was always ridiculed and labelled a Bad Child, and made to believe that this would ruin his life.

Since childhood, we have been made to fear our Parents, Teachers, basically any higher authority. So whenver a person of higher authority does something wrong which affects us, we immediately go into "Fear State" and quietly suffer as no one can stop anything bad happening to themselves.

As the Indian way of Parenting teaches submission and obedience, there was never a situation where the child had to make a decision for himself, and when he grows up and has to face the real world, he feels unprepared. For the child now an adult, it is an unfamiliar situation as they never got to choose for themselves since their childhood. Everything was either spoonfed to them or forced onto them, which registered basic freedom in their brain as something forbidden or dangerous

As older generations pass by and newer generations take its place, it is now the responsibility of the new generation, i.e., us to ditch the old and ineffective methods of parenting and use effective measures. Let's raise a generation of thinkers, and not slaves.
